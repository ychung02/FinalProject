Title: Diary

Used: Sublime Text

Description: This is the Score project that was done in 2015 Relational Design, under Dinah Fried's guidance. Instruction --- (1) Click the black area on the left, as many times as you want. (2) Write your own diary with the words you got. (3) The length, mood, subject, or changing present tense to past tense is all up to you (and you don’t have to repeat the word that gets repeated).

The main subject of this website is empathy. Left side of the webpage is filled with Alice's personal diary during 2015, without nouns. It is also not following the proper translation order, through fragmentation. The performer eventually goes through the process of recalling his or her ideas, experiences and memories in order to connect himself or herself with the word he or she got. Via this process, both of the performer and the composer(Alice) can realize that he or she is not the only one who goes through this certain emotion, idea, or moment in life. Try it!!!

Title: Priceless Toilet

Used: Sublime Text

Description: This is the Opinion project that was done in 2015 Relational Design, under Dinah Fried's guidance. It displays scanned images of the survey that was done in Providence Place Mall, during March 7,2015(from 2pm to 5pm) by Alice Sun.

Providence is a quite academic place, which has RISD and Brown; and they often share conceptual ideas in many criteria. It’s definitely a positive phenomenon, yet there’s also a risk to be trapped in a closed world. It’s easy for them to ignore those who aren’t in this world, because they are “privileged” artists and technologists. Thus, Alice did a survey with the subject "Marcel Duchamp's Fountain" at Providence Place Mall, which isn’t particularly filled with educated or academic people. Then she scanned the survey and rearranged in this form, allowing viewers to compare three sets of opinions. Through this form, these voices can interact with each other, and sometimes can be the answer to one another. Try it!!!

"privileged" knowledge

Title: UTC Timezone

Software: Adobe Illustrator, Indesign

Description: UTC Timezone (folderable) Map was done in 2014 Making Meaning class under Lucinda Hitchcock's guidance. It visualizes world map only through UTC Timezone division, with the names of well known cities in order to provide a better sense of location. Although it is usually kept flat, it can be folded or be bent; and in that case, those borders that go across another timezone will be bulge out. The poster also focuses on those "bulge out" parts, with some information about UTC Timezone.

Alice's initial approach of this mapping project grew out from her previous work during freshman year: What is time?; Is time a logical thing?; How certain rules or standards were built? UTC Timezone is the good example that tried to be rational but eventually became out of control, due to political or geographical factors. Originally, UTC timezones tend to follow longitude, although none of them fully follows it. This irregular image of time, which people depend on doubtlessly, intrigued Alice.

Title: Graphis Poster

Software: Adobe Photoshop, Indesign

Description: This project was done in History of Grophic Design 2014 class by Douglas Scott, under Michael McDermott's guidance. The class was mostly lecture-based by Doug, and two different topics were given for each student to make one timeline accordian book and one poster.

This a 17 in. x 34 in. poster about Graphis Magazine and its founder, Walter Herdeg. Walter Herdeg always aimed to explain abstract facts or functions through visual expression; no wonder, his magazines also appear to collect various works of art from all criteria with the same purpose. Based on this information, Alice decided to create "space" in the poster, which is also an abstract subject, by collaging some images from the magazines. The main stage is filled with an essay about Graphis Magazine and Walter Herdeg written by Alice.

Title: Muriel Cooper Timeline

Software: Adobe Photoshop, Indesign

Description: This project was done in History of Grophic Design 2014 class by Douglas Scott, under Michael McDermott's guidance. The class was mostly lecture-based by Doug, and two different topics were given for each student to make one timeline accordian book and one poster.

This is an accordian book that visualizes Muriel Cooper's life in three categories: life, art/design, and historical. Each category is marked with one of RGB colors; life events were marked with red, art/design events with green, and historical events with blue. Many times these events influenced one another, and in these cases a color gradient was applied. The overall design was originated from Muriel Coooper's life itself, which was dedicated for graphic design, especially as one of the earliest designer who actively used computer technology.

Title: KOAR

Software: Adobe Photoshop, Indesign, Illustrator, Premiere Pro

Description: This is a rebranding project that was done in Franz Werner's Form and Communication class 2013.

KOAR is a short term of Korean Air, which is one of the well known transportation companies, especially in Korea. Title: Homage to Oscar Niemeyer

Software: Rhino 3D

Description: What's the border between fine art and design? Under the guidance of professor Kai Franz, students were asked to do figure croquis with Rhino 3D. Rhino 3D is a software that is generally used for industrial design or architecture, however, Kai broken that boundary and allowed Alice to experience digital art in a new way. After that, instead of painting, which she was originally planing to major in, she decided to go for graphic design; because she found infinite possibilities in using computer- not only as a mere tool, but also as the most flexible medium.

The process simply begined with selecting and researching about any modern architect; in Alice's case, Oscar Niemeyer. The next step was to choose a certain architecture by the architect and reinterpret it. Alice chose to work on Oscar Niemeyer Museum, which is characterized by its unique eye-like geometric shape.

Title: Collage "Zero-gravity"

Software: Rhino 3D, Adobe Illustrator CS

Description: What's the border between fine art and design? Under the guidance of professor Kai Franz, students were asked to do figure croquis with Rhino 3D. Rhino 3D is a software that is generally used for industrial design or architecture, however, Kai broken that boundary and allowed Alice to experience digital art in a new way. After that, instead of painting, which she was originally planing to major in, she decided to go for graphic design; because she found infinite possibilities in using computer- not only as a mere tool, but also as the most flexible medium.

As a final form, all the 8 croquis were used to create a 24 in. x 34 in. collage poster. It went through three fundamental process that she found through her RISD foundation year, which is 'understanding-deconstructing-reconstructing' a subject. The experience of discovering the new aspects of digital art became her 'understanding' part, and via 'deconstructing' and 'reconstructing' those existing images, she ended up with this abstract collage. It contains her idea of how digital mediums are eligible of liberating artists from numerous limitations, from 'gravity' = realistic obstacles.
